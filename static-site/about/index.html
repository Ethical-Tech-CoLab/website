About

Exploring technology to improve the human condition.

The NYU Center for Global Affairs launched the Ethical Tech CoLab to focus on interventions in the human condition using emerging technologies, through interdisciplinary applied research that draws on students, faculty, and partners from across NYU and industry.

Achieving full human potential through technology.

We change the conversation on how people are informed and how emerging technology is used for good — translating frontier research into working tools that institutions and communities can actually use, from speculative possibility to deployable intervention on migration, forced labor, and the rights of displaced people.

Technology shaped by the people it affects, not the other way around.

Humans should be able to own their digital identity and credentials regardless of regime change, and emerging tech, used well, can measurably reduce the volume of forced labor and the precarity of refugees and IDPs.

Lab Director

Prof. Yorke E. Rhodes III

NYU CGA Global Economy · Cofounder of Blockchain at Microsoft · Teaching at NYU for 12 years.

Professor Rhodes currently teaches "Beyond the Brand: Harnessing Emerging Technology in Ethical Sourcing" at CGA, and works on traceability for forced labor compliance.

Earlier in his career, he was an initial funding partner of ID2020, an NGO focused on decentralized humanitarian identity for refugees and displaced people in partnership with UNHCR and the World Food Program, founded during the first Syrian refugee crisis on the premise that people should be able to prove their skills, history, and education across borders.

The CoLab brings together his cross-organizational work in Forced Labor and Human Trafficking with Microsoft Tech for Fundamental Rights, and collaborations with Microsoft on proof-of-human, AI, web3, censorship-resistant security, and privacy-preserving technologies such as zero-knowledge proofs.

What we do

Research, prototypes, publication.

01

Research & investigation design

Papers and proposals on using AI and emerging tech for preventative interventions and awareness of forced labor.

02

Source evaluation

Investigate and prepare data sources for interventions: job postings, online game streams, chat rooms, supply-chain signals.

03

Proofs of concept

Build PoCs that test the effectiveness of data sources, intervention tactics, and awareness campaigns.

04

Publish & scale

Document interventions and propose scaled-up tests with the goal of meaningfully reducing the volume of forced labor.

Principles

How we work.

01

Field-grounded

Every project begins with the practitioners and communities who will live with the outcome.

02

Prototype-first

We ship working systems, not white papers, and we measure them in real conditions.

03

Open by default

Methods, evaluations, and lessons are shared so others can extend the work.
